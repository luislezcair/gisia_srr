How To Brew

Iced Pour-Over

\n\nIced pour-over is a simple and approachable way to make rich, full-flavored iced coffee one pitcher at a time.\n\n* Coffee Press\n* Pour-Over\n* Iced Pour-Over\n* Coffee Brewer\n*\n\n

STEP-BY-STEP

\n\n*\n\nPre-moisten filter\n\nBriefly rinse the paper filter with hot water, and discard the rinse water. This will give your coffee the purest flavor possible.\n\n*\n\nChoose the right grind\n\nFor pour-over, use coffee ground for a paper cone. It\u2019s a relatively fine grind that looks like granulated sugar.\n\n*\n\nMeasure\n\nIced pour-over is brewed at double strength and diluted with ice, so measure 4 tablespoons of coffee for every 6 ounces of hot water.\n\n*\n\nPour and pause\n\nUse hot water that\u2019s just off the boil and fill the cone halfway to saturate the grounds. Pause for 10 seconds and let the coffee bloom. This lets it hydrate evenly and begin to develop flavor.\n\n*\n\nComplete the pour\n\nSlowly add the rest of the water, pouring in small, steady circles to cover all the grounds.\n\n*\n\nAdd ice\n\nPour your brewed coffee over fresh ice to chill, and serve immediately.\n\nTIP:\nAfrican coffees are popular for iced pour-over because of their superb juicy body, and tangy, sparkling citrus notes.\n\n*\n\nUse the Starbucks\u00ae Pour-Over Iced Coffee Brewer to make a pitcher of iced coffee in minutes.
